## 🏢 Business Problem

> High growth does not always translate into profitability.

Between FY23 and FY26, Zomato expanded aggressively across multiple business verticals including Food Delivery, Blinkit, Hyperpure, and Going Out.

The challenge was understanding:

- What drove Zomato's profitability turnaround?
- Which businesses contributed most to growth?
- How important was Blinkit in reshaping the business?
- Is the current growth trajectory sustainable?
- What should management focus on next?

### Objective

Identify the key drivers behind Zomato's transformation from a loss-making company into a profitable and diversified business ecosystem.

- Improve order economics

#### 2️⃣ Protect Food Delivery Margins

- Improve operational efficiency
- Increase customer retention
- Maintain profitability discipline

#### 3️⃣ Scale Hyperpure

- Expand merchant partnerships
- Grow recurring B2B revenue
- Strengthen supply-chain advantages

#### 4️⃣ Increase Ecosystem Synergies

- Improve cross-platform engagement
- Increase customer lifetime value
- Strengthen ecosystem stickiness

> 💡 **Executive Takeaway:** Sustainable growth will depend on balancing Blinkit's expansion with profitability discipline across the broader ecosystem.

## 🏆 Final Conclusion

> **Zomato's transformation was not driven by cost-cutting alone.**

It was driven by a combination of:

✅ Revenue expansion

✅ Margin improvement

✅ Business diversification

✅ Blinkit's rapid scale-up

✅ Strong execution across multiple growth engines

What began as a food delivery company evolved into a diversified platform capable of sustaining long-term profitable growth.

### Final Management Takeaway

- Continue profitable Blinkit expansion
- Protect Food Delivery margins
- Scale Hyperpure as a recurring growth engine
- Increase ecosystem synergies across businesses

> **A case study in disciplined scaling, strategic diversification, and profitable growth.**
